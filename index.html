M. Goss is a multidsciplinary
creative currently presiding
in Bellingham, Washington.

“M” Goss is the visionary millennial taking the Bellingham art scene by storm. A renowned botanist, philosopher, and modern spiritualist, he is best known for his 'deeply disturbing critiques on the epicurean aesthetic of the twenty first century'. His undergraduate work primarily consists of an exploration of "pseudo epistemological solipsism" through periodic spells of unconsciousness via drug induced psychosis and has been called 'riveting' and 'haunting'. His seminal debut novel "633B", a bleak traipse into the myopic existence of three boys and a ghost on the cusp of adulthood, is widely considered to be the definitive novel of his generation.
